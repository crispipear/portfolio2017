Problem

Climate change is a global issue in our society. While the issue has brought many environmental impacts to the planet we are living on, it also brought social problems to the community. It is not apparent to the public that the impacts of climate change is not the same for everyone. The Climate Gap is a social problem where existing social economic, and health disparities mean that racial minorities and low-income communities are both more likely to be affected by and have a harder time adapting to new climate realities

Communities who are impacted by the climate gap are vulnerable to the impacts of climate change due to social and economic status. For example, the low-income demographic groups will less likely to afford housing in communities that are cleaner with ammenties to relieve heat waves.

Research source by Got Green.

Challenge

The course require us in teams of ten people, create an alternate reality game that centered on the theme of intersectional approaches to climate change. We are required to design the game using UW Bothell campus as the location and design interactive elements for the audience to participate in.

What is "Murder in Imdy"?

"Murder in Imdy" is an alternate reality game that encourages the player to solve a murder happened in a fictional town "Imdy" over the death of Tobias Kowalski (fictional character). Players will unravel the story behind the murder mystery not only to discover the truth of his death, but also the social impacts on the people of Imdy due to corporations malpractice and climate change.

Marketing Strategies

The three main mediums for the game marketing campaign are website, printed media and social media. Instead of bluntly telling the public to play the game, we hope to utilize the game narrative in all the marketing mediums. Our main goal for the marketing campaign is to engage the public audience and create a more immersive experience even before the they get to play the game. The public audience will be able to get the previews of the game narrative and gameplay hints through the marketing campaign.

Social Media

Objective: Immerse players with the perspectives of the fictional characters.

Instagram: Marketing team will be posting as Tobi (fictional character, victim) through a timeline up until his day of death. The posts on the instagram will be used as a hint of locations that the victim has attended as a source of investigation. Tobi will also post like other daily life events to create a more realistic vibe on the character.

Twitter: Marketing team will be posting as Nadia (fictional character, victim’s family member). Similar to Tobi, Nadia’s account will also be posting daily life events to create a more realistic feel to the character. There will be posts about Tobi’s death and rants on seeking for truth. The twitter account will be used to engage emotional connection between the players and the game narrative.
